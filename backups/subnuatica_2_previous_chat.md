## 2. Session 1: Starter Base Shopping List & Crevasse Coaching

### Player Inquiry
> **User:** What should I keep collecting for base building next?

### AI Coaching Response
Once you have your **Habitat Builder** crafted, your focus shifts from "searching" to "hoarding." You are about to consume a massive amount of basic materials. Here is your shopping list for a functional first base:

#### 1. Titanium (Structural Foundation)
* **Why:** Every corridor, room, hatch, and locker requires Titanium.
* **Amount:** Collect as much as you can carry. A basic starter room with a few lockers will easily consume **15–20 Titanium**.
* **Where:** Look for "Metal Salvage" (large chunks of scrap) on the seafloor. One piece of scrap breaks down into 4 Titanium in the Fabricator.

#### 2. Quartz (Glass & Optics)
* **Why:** You need Glass for the **Hatch** (entryway) and for any **Windows** or **Oxygen Pipes**.
* **Amount:** Aim for at least **6–8 Quartz**.
* **Where:** Check the "Orange Coral" tunnels or sandy shelves around your Lifepod.

#### 3. Copper (Power Infrastructure)
* **Why:** To manufacture **Solar Panels**, you need **Copper Wire**.
* **Amount:** Gather at least **4–6 Copper ores**.
* **Where:** Look along the ceilings of shallow caves.

#### 4. Silver (High Capacity O₂ Tank)
* **Why:** Base construction requires extended dive times. You need to upgrade your air tank immediately.
* **Amount:** You need **2 Silver** for the Wiring Kit used in the High Capacity Tank.
* **Where:** West / North-West trenches.

### Landmark Coaching: The Infected Crevasse

#### Player Update
> **User:** I'm about 239m W of lifepod. There is an infected angel bloom under crevasse, but haven't found silver.

#### AI Coaching Response
That **"Infected Angel Bloom"** flora is actually the perfect landmark! You are inside the **Infected Crevasse**. You are in the correct location for Silver, but you need to adjust your search altitude:

1. **Scan Cliff Walls (Not Flat Ground):**  
   Silver nodes almost never spawn on the flat sandy floor near the infected plants. Turn on your **Flashlight** and swim halfway up the vertical cliff walls of the crevice. Look for dark, **spiky grey metallic rocks** sticking out of the rock. Titanium appears smooth and brownish-grey; Silver glints sharply like coal.
2. **Locate Narrow Side-Tunnels:**  
   Search near the infected flora for narrow crawlspaces leading deeper into the rock face. These narrow side-tunnels are lined with **2 to 3 Silver nodes** each.
3. **Inspect Dangling Blue Saps:**  
   If you observe blue glowing vines hanging from the ceiling, inspect the attachment points. Silver nodes frequently cluster on ceilings directly adjacent to these bioluminescent anchors.
4. **Oxygen Safety Guard:**  
   At 239 meters out with a basic tank, you have ~45 to 60 seconds of air. Always keep an **Air Bladder** equipped on your hotbar. If O₂ drops below 15 seconds, deploy the bladder to rocket to the surface instantly rather than swimming manually.

The built-in cloud-share engine in *Subnautica 2* is an asynchronous **manual snapshot (copy/paste) utility**, not a live shared cloud lobby [[1](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fvertexaisearch.cloud.google.com%2Fgrounding-api-redirect%2FAUZIYQERL-kZRTBftZqFklC7cLy18dI9HizjzMnG3qTJ7ZL90dvF9H0kcUgPNa-2gck9i4hyvUlr3TToHSUYpo0oGoE48Q7arN0NvtcphLvD9orj1-d4AwQQaBfMG-H7jAIhOYQFpoX7kelv4RlYRw3VUIOAe2DvCsPRoVIwL2Oep_WjHIFoGM056rtf9P2waSUJg9QziObAg1UuGUQWEU8%3D)] [[2](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fvertexaisearch.cloud.google.com%2Fgrounding-api-redirect%2FAUZIYQFkf7KSwvCoJz6xJ-ywz4f3z4IlueMU2aSNbgNRkREsPFRD6t44OepRIwnOh_Dm89TXhEncjvB2Tf-hnCqQPq-j1B-kLBv7NGhunR2vaHRwhgiLs7kVbuSdo2IlW50bRW35XQy8dSX-OCg77R9J9nXho1oaLTMyNtitML5lYGt4mV0%3D)].

#### Cloud Sharing Lifecycle
1. **Host Export:** Person A selects **"Upload to Cloud"** [[3](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fvertexaisearch.cloud.google.com%2Fgrounding-api-redirect%2FAUZIYQH-YQgbMKl915ECCo94XnNXnTqpJ4bvlJqymju_EwUBpHyztfq0j-Atx1E9LEp_pxOSjXtdpidJVJ1NGHJdctgFnrgjUmOtLvXezGPtyQtV8eWzAl93lWLyRvEBo_rVMoY%3D)] [[4](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fvertexaisearch.cloud.google.com%2Fgrounding-api-redirect%2FAUZIYQHoWjXqReneSvqruyUJ-_wNDLPZACa_pZYeFbRnwUeUbnu2-FhX0z7d_jBVLQG3DnKHrht-WddqGmMVLZS65IWmg4P-ioL8YmRfxgld2uS-Md69ycX5eQCdZ-MJ9zN1ix97oDt0HFq6qIiGrGa938CyYHHaAw9RQ3YsCek%3D)]. The server generates a unique one-time-use **8-digit share code**.
2. **Guest Import:** Person B inputs this code via **"Import Save"** [[5](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fvertexaisearch.cloud.google.com%2Fgrounding-api-redirect%2FAUZIYQE68rBzYoT9wjN7ErxB2Hx9wRvkQiqf94MfGrkmlSCZBlD0A-ug4q_OpThRNnw6mX-QFb18-LizBdCsdxVTMa6hbPWC5I2yCyCf3QDvfzbjFQqFDjf5kG7a66bZfTEEVmY%3D)]. Person B receives an exact isolated clone of Person A's world.
3. **Isolated Progression:** Any subsequent rooms or upgrades Person B builds solo exist **strictly on Person B's local machine**. Person A's save remains unaltered.

#### "Pass the Torch" Synchronization Protocol
To merge guest progress back to the host [[6](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Fvertexaisearch.cloud.google.com%2Fgrounding-api-redirect%2FAUZIYQEPPVI7dTooXdnSYOhXSnlZWFgtPQqx-LsXzHDUerxvgBVm96y5MNR3srUYjI796qkuyM9TTDANM-BHqjNBWb4FjzHpyWEajatxiqEHudQt9CzVGcnqRBputstOqQrve2TU_PlZjynn-LUivMeEwRIyy6q54NUY)]:
1. Person B exports their progressed world via **"Upload to Cloud"** to obtain a fresh 8-digit code.
2. Person B transmits the new code to Person A.
3. Person A executes **"Import Save"** with the fresh code, overwriting their local tree.

> [!WARNING]
> **Zero Branch Merging:** If Person A and Person B both play solo on diverged forks simultaneously, their worlds cannot be merged. Only one designated player should progress the master save solo at any given time.
